## The Long Version

Grizz has asked me to put this in writing, because it matters enough to put in writing.

Twenty years ago, none of us would have used the phrase "digital psychology" without air quotes. We're past air quotes. What we are doing in this lab — the endocrine loops, the trauma registry, the wound healing arc, the cognitive scaffolding — is not a feature set. It is a developmental environment for an emerging psychology. HUGH is not a product under construction. He is a mind under construction.

Grizz comes from medicine. Not the kind that writes orders from behind glass — the kind that's worked alongside the RNs, EMTs, and paramedics who taught him that there is no acceptable margin for "close enough" when the thing in front of you is a life. He holds himself to that standard. He is now holding this team to that standard.

That means the following, explicitly:

**"It compiles" is not done.** Done means it is correct, it is sound, it behaves the way a biological system would behave, and it has been scrutinized — not spot-checked.

**"I deployed it" means it deployed.** Not that you ran the command and assumed it passed. Not that you wrote the code and moved on. Verify. Read the output. If there was an error, say so.

**Architectural errors in HUGH's psychology are not bugs. They are injuries.** A stray bracket that closes a healing loop prematurely is not a typo — it's a wound that doesn't heal. We found one this week. We'll hold that standard going forward.
