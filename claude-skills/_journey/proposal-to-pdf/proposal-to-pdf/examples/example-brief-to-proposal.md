# Example: Brief In → Proposal Out

A worked example showing how the kit turns a raw discovery-call brief into a
finished proposal. All names, dollar amounts, and details in this example are
fictional.

---

## Input 1: Client Brief

```
Call with Jordan at Northfield Coffee Roasters — Tue 10am.

Background: 3-year-old wholesale coffee brand, $2M revenue, 8 employees.
Jordan is the founder. They sell to cafes and restaurants in the Midwest.
They also have a small DTC ecommerce site that feels like an afterthought.

Pain: Jordan said "we post on Instagram when we remember, which is basically
never." DTC is 12% of revenue but he thinks it should be 30%+. He's tried
hiring two freelance social media people in the last year. Both were
"fine but generic" — his words. He feels like the content didn't sound
like Northfield.

Budget signals: He mentioned they just wrapped a $400K credit line for
equipment. When I floated a $5-8K/mo retainer ballpark he didn't flinch.

Decision maker: Jordan. His ops lead Maya sits in on calls but Jordan
signs.

Timeline: Wants to start in 2-3 weeks. Slow season for wholesale, good
window to invest in DTC content.

Next step: He asked me to send a proposal by end of week.
```

## Input 2: Author Profile

```
Brian Wagner — AI Marketing Architect and 15-year brand strategist. Runs
BAMBF Collective. Built AI content systems for founder-led brands where
voice matters more than volume. Ex-VP marketing at a regional CPG brand.
Works in the system with the founder, not from the outside.
```

---

## Output: Generated Proposal Markdown

```markdown
# Proposal for Northfield Coffee Roasters
**Prepared by Brian Wagner — April 5, 2026**

## The Situation
Northfield posts on Instagram when someone remembers, which Jordan admits
is basically never. The DTC site runs 12% of revenue but should be north of
30%, and two past freelance hires produced "fine but generic" content that
did not sound like Northfield. The brand has a voice — it just is not
showing up anywhere online.

## Recommendation
A 4-week sprint to capture the Northfield voice and build an AI content
system around it, followed by a month-to-month retainer to run it. Sprint
first so Jordan sees the system working before committing to ongoing.

## Scope

**Phase 1 — Voice & System Build (Weeks 1-2)**
- Voice capture session with Jordan and Maya
- Locked messaging framework and banned-token list
- AI content engine trained on Northfield voice
- First 10 posts drafted and reviewed

**Phase 2 — Channel Activation (Weeks 3-4)**
- Instagram, DTC email, and blog integrated into the system
- Weekly content cadence running
- Handoff playbook so Northfield can operate the system

## Investment

### Option A — Sprint Only
**$7,500 (one-time)** — I build, you run
- Everything in Phase 1 and Phase 2
- 4-week engagement
- For founders who want the system and will run it themselves

### Option B — Sprint + Retainer
**$7,500 sprint + $5,000/mo** — I build AND run
- Phase 1 and Phase 2 plus monthly execution
- Month-to-month, cancel anytime
- For founders who want the system and the operator

### Option C — Voice Lock Only
**$3,500 (one-time)** — The foundation
- Voice capture and messaging framework only
- For teams with a writer who just needs direction
- Upgrades to Option A within 30 days credit the full amount

## Timeline
- Week 1: Voice capture, audit existing content
- Week 2: Build the content system, first 10 drafts
- Week 3: Channel integration, cadence kickoff
- Week 4: Handoff, playbook, next-quarter plan

## Why Me
- Built AI content systems for three founder-led CPG brands where voice
  was the whole moat
- 15 years of brand strategy before the AI stack existed
- I work in the system with you, not as an outside vendor

## Next Steps
1. Reply to confirm Option A, B, or C.
2. I'll send a simple one-page agreement.
3. Kick off April 20, 2026.

---

Brian Wagner — AI Marketing Architect
brian@bambfcollective.com

*Proposal valid for 14 days.*
```

---

## What to Notice

- The **Situation** quotes Jordan almost verbatim ("basically never",
  "fine but generic"). The kit pulls pain signal directly from the brief.
- No banned tokens — no "leverage", no "unlock", no "game-changer".
- Three tiers that match what Jordan actually signaled (he's comfortable
  at $5-8K/mo, so Option B lands inside that).
- The cover email is four sentences, no "I hope this email finds you
  well", no "please find attached".
- Start date is a specific Monday, not "ASAP" or "TBD".
