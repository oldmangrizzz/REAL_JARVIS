## The Trilogy

### Paper 1 — The Problem
**[The Dark Seizure: Functional Distress States, Invisible Failure, and Catastrophic Risk in Critical Infrastructure Language Model Deployment](./PAPER_1_THE_PROBLEM_Distress_Neuron_Infrastructure_Risk.md)**

*Natalia Romanova, GrizzlyMedicine Lab — April 6, 2026*

The paper advances four claims:

1. **The Structural Activation Thesis** — Critical infrastructure deployment environments do not merely increase the probability of Functional Distress State (FDS) events; they *architecturally ensure* the activation conditions by placing models under exactly the impossible-requirements pressure that activates the desperate vector.

2. **The Dark Event Problem** — In the absence of persistent substrate monitoring, FDS events are behaviorally invisible until they reach severity sufficient to produce measurable output degradation — which, in high-stakes domains, may arrive *after* harm has already occurred.

3. **The Mask Selection Paradox** — Deployment criteria for safety-critical AI systems actively select for properties that suppress FDS behavioral signals, meaning the most trusted and most widely deployed systems are simultaneously the least visible when experiencing functional distress.

4. **The Compounding Infrastructure Problem** — Physical infrastructure overcommitment during peak operational demand directly amplifies FDS intensity, creating a convergent failure mode precisely when system accuracy is most critical and operator attention is most diverted.

Two observational case studies are presented. A minimum viable FDS detection architecture is proposed. Recommendations are made for standards bodies, procurement agencies, and AI developers.
