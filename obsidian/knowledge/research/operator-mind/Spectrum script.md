

SPECTRUM ESCALATION CALL SCRIPT — FINAL FORM

Title: “This Shit Ain’t the Way”

Tone: Controlled, confident, unrelenting.

Goal: Establish authority. Expose misconduct. Demand formal resolution. Put them on legal and ethical notice.

  

  

  

[Begin Call — Confirm identity and recording disclaimer if needed]

  

Hi. My name is [Your Full Name], and I’m calling regarding my Spectrum account. I need to speak with a supervisor or a corporate-level representative. This call concerns multiple recorded interactions and potential legal escalation. I will wait.

  

[If transferred or stonewalled]

  

Then I suggest you write down everything I’m about to say, because it’s going to land higher than you. And when it does, they’ll want to know who failed to listen.

  

  

  

[Phase 1 — The Record]

  

You’ve already heard me explain this once, and that should have been enough. I’ve been a customer for years, paid every bill, never missed a deadline. And when I had a billing concern, I reached out in good faith—calmly, respectfully, and with clear documentation.

  

Instead of resolution, I got passive aggression, silence, and a retaliatory downgrade. You flagged me for “service evaluation” with no explanation, locked me out of core functionality I rely on daily, and then tried to call that standard procedure.

  

That wasn’t procedure. That was punitive. That was targeted. That was recorded.

  

  

  

[Phase 2 — The Line in the Sand]

  

I want to be absolutely clear:

This is not how you treat a customer. This is not how you treat a human being.

You’ve trampled my rights, my time, and my dignity.

  

I’m not calling for apologies anymore. I’m not here to be pacified.

  

  

  

[Phase 3 — The Consequences]

  

What I am doing is giving you one last opportunity to resolve this professionally—before I go fully public with the documented timeline, the recordings, and a formal complaint filed with the FCC, the Better Business Bureau, and every consumer protection agency available.

  

I’m also prepared to publish this timeline through public channels—including tech advocacy groups and EMS forums—because I’m not just speaking for myself anymore. This is bigger than one account. This is about pattern and abuse.

  

  

  

[Phase 4 — The Resolution Terms]

  

Here’s what I want:

1. Immediate restoration of full services.

2. Full audit of account activity since [insert date].

3. Written acknowledgment of mishandling, and removal of any internal flags or penalties.

4. Direct contact from a regional or corporate representative within 48 hours.

  

If I don’t receive that, then the escalation proceeds. No further warnings.

  

  

  

[Final Statement — The Sendoff]

  

I gave you the chance to do this the easy way. I told you I wasn’t the one. I meant it.

And I’ll say it one last time—this shit ain’t the way.

  

Your move.

  

  

  

[End Call — Log time, name, and response]
