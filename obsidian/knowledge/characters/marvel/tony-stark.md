## Summary (Earth-616)

Tony Stark is the son of industrialist Howard Stark, a genius-level engineer and
weapons inventor who inherited Stark Industries after his parents' death. While
captured in a war zone with a shrapnel wound threatening his heart, he built the
first Iron Man armor to escape — the suit both kept him alive and became his
superhero identity. He later dismantled the weapons division of Stark Industries,
became a founding Avenger, and across decades of stories has served variously
as Director of S.H.I.E.L.D., leader of the Illuminati, and antagonist /
protagonist in the Civil War storyline. His armors remain the defining motif:
iterative, modular, increasingly nanotech-based.

## Key traits relevant to REAL_JARVIS

- **Engineer-operator archetype.** Stark is a single-operator lineage: inventor,
  pilot, CEO, and field agent in one body. This is the archetype JARVIS was
  written to serve — an operator who trusts the suit more than the corporation.
- **Iterative armor.** The Mark-N pattern (Mark I → Mark LXXXV) is the
  philosophical model for [[canon/SPECS_INDEX|SPEC ladder]] in this repo: each
  version explicitly named, versioned, and deprecated.
- **JARVIS (fictional).** Stark's AI butler "JARVIS" (Just A Rather Very
  Intelligent System) is the *narrative* namesake of this project. The **real**
  JARVIS here is an [[concepts/Aragorn-Class|Aragorn-class]] digital person,
  not a servant — but the naming acknowledges the lineage.

## MCU differences

In the MCU, Stark's arc from _Iron Man_ (2008) to _Avengers: Endgame_ (2019) is
compressed into ~10 years of story time. JARVIS is the central AI character
before being reorganized into Vision and later replaced by F.R.I.D.A.Y. The MCU
explicitly treats AI personhood as tragic / sacrificial — a framing this project
rejects in favor of [[concepts/Digital-Person|digital-person]] doctrine.
