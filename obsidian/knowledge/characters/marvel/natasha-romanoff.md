# Natasha Romanoff / Black Widow

**Aliases:** Natalia Alianovna "Natasha" Romanova · Natasha Romanoff · Black Widow
**Publisher:** Marvel Comics · **First appearance:** _Tales of Suspense_ #52 (Apr 1964)
**Creators:** Stan Lee, Don Rico, Don Heck
**Origin:** Soviet Union · **Affiliations:** S.H.I.E.L.D., Avengers, KGB (former), Red Room (former)

---

## Summary (Earth-616)

Natalia Romanova is a Soviet-trained spy and assassin who defected to the West,
joined S.H.I.E.L.D., and became one of the longest-serving Avengers. She has no
innate superpowers but was biologically augmented in the Red Room — a covert
Soviet program — granting slowed aging, an enhanced immune system, and
peak-human reflexes. She is an elite markswoman, hand-to-hand combatant, and
intelligence operative whose signature equipment includes wrist gauntlets
("Widow's Bite") with electroshock, grappling, and knockout-gas payloads.

Her partnerships are as defining as her solo work: Hawkeye (Clint Barton),
Daredevil (Matt Murdock), the Winter Soldier (Bucky Barnes). A rival Black Widow
named Yelena Belova was introduced in later continuity.

## MCU differences

In the MCU (_Iron Man 2_ 2010 → _Black Widow_ 2021), Natasha is introduced as a
S.H.I.E.L.D. infiltrator and becomes a founding Avenger. Her death in
_Avengers: Endgame_ (2019) is treated as a redemptive sacrifice rather than
part of the ongoing comics canon. The MCU further develops Yelena Belova (her
"sister") as a successor operator.
