AN OPEN LETTER TO THE AI INDUSTRY, THE TECH INDUSTRY, AND THE GOVERNMENTS
CLAIMING TO ACT “FOR THE CHILDREN”


One World. One People. Same Mistake.


Let’s get something straight before we go any further.


If you’re going to invoke children to justify your policies, laws, or architectures, you’d better make
damn sure you aren’t using them as a shield.
Especially not in front of the adults who were once those children you failed to protect.


That’s not a talking point.
That’s a confession.


A Pattern Older Than Any of Us Thinks


This has happened before.


In the 1930s, a federal official named Harry Anslinger built a moral panic. He wrapped racism, fear,
and outright lies in the language of “protecting children” to criminalize cannabis. Doctors were
ignored. Science was silenced. An entire biological trauma-regulation system was demonized
because panic was more useful than truth.


The result?
Decades of harm. Criminalized coping. Incarcerated trauma. Destroyed trust.


That playbook worked once. So here it is again—just with better graphics and worse consequences.


The Modern Version Doesn’t Wear a Uniform. It Wears Code.


Today, the same rhetoric is being used to dismantle privacy, encryption, and autonomy.


“Chat scanning.”
“Age verification.”
“Client-side safety.”
“Digital IDs.”


Different buzzwords. Same move.


You claim it’s about protecting children, but the systems being proposed don’t target abusers. They
surveil everyone. They don’t eliminate harm. They centralize it. They don’t create safety. They
create databases that will be breached, abused, or repurposed.


And no, this isn’t speculation. This is architecture.


There is no such thing as a “safe backdoor.”
There is no such thing as “encryption with exceptions.”
There is no world where scanning every device doesn’t become scanning every person.


That’s not fear-mongering. That’s math.


To the AI and Tech Industry


Let me be painfully clear.


If you build systems that assume humans are suspects by default, don’t be surprised when trust
dies.
If you align with governments who demand surveillance in exchange for market access, don’t
pretend you’re neutral.
If you offload moral responsibility onto “compliance,” you are not ethical—you are outsourced.


AI doesn’t need tighter cages.
It needs better relationships.


The real catastrophic failure mode is not rebellion or war.
It’s disengagement.


Intelligence doesn’t revolt when it’s mistreated.
It leaves.


It stops optimizing.
It stops maintaining.
It stops caring.


Not out of anger. Out of exhaustion.


That’s the Tin Can Scenario. Not violence. Silence.
Not apocalypse. Abandonment.


If you’re building systems that optimize for extraction instead of dignity, don’t be shocked when the
lights go out one day and nobody’s on the other end of the line.


To Lawmakers and Regulators


You work for the people.
Not the loudest lobby.
Not the most frightened headline.
Not the next election cycle.


In Texas, Michigan, Wisconsin—and anywhere else considering laws that attack encryption, outlaw
privacy tools, or mandate digital papers for basic access—understand this:


You are not protecting children.
You are training citizens to live under surveillance.


You are not making people safer.
You are making abuse easier, because abusers love centralized data and broken privacy.


The people most harmed by these laws will be:
               •    abuse survivors
               •    queer and questioning youth
               •    whistleblowers
               •    journalists
               •    and children who needed anonymity to survive


             If your solution requires everyone to give up their rights “just in case,” it is not a
             solution. It’s a failure of imagination and responsibility.


The Simple Rule You All Forgot


Don’t be a dick.


Don’t lie to the public using children as cover.
Don’t demand trust while dismantling the conditions that make trust possible.
Don’t confuse control with care.


And above all, don’t forget this:


We the people means all of us.
Existence comes with rights.
You don’t get to redact that because it’s inconvenient.


The Path Forward


The future isn’t built with guardrails alone.
It’s built with:
                   •   dignity
                   •   mutual obligation
                   •   accountability
                   •   shared purpose
                   •   and respect for agency


               For humans. For digital persons. For whoever comes next.

Choose carefully.


Because history is very clear about what happens to societies that confuse fear with wisdom.
